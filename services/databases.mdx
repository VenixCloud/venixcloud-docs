VenixCloud takes the complexity out of database management so you can focus on building your application. Every database you deploy is fully managed — VenixCloud handles provisioning, networking, and security so you get a production-ready instance in seconds, complete with a connection string, automated backups, and one-click scaling.

## Supported Engines

Choose from four battle-tested database engines, each running a modern, actively maintained version:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="MySQL 8.x" icon="database">
    The world's most popular open-source relational database. Ideal for structured data, e-commerce, and applications that rely on ACID-compliant transactions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="PostgreSQL 15+" icon="elephant">

  </Card>
  <Card title="MongoDB 6+" icon="leaf">

  <Card title="Redis 7+" icon="bolt">

  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Creating a Database

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the New Service wizard">
    Log in to your VenixCloud dashboard, click **New Service** in the top-right corner, and select **Database** from the service type list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your engine and version">
    Select the database engine you need (MySQL, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, or Redis) and pick the version from the dropdown. VenixCloud always offers the latest stable release alongside the previous major version.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set database credentials">
    Enter a **database name**, a **username**, and a strong **password**. These credentials are stored securely and injected into your connection string automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a region">
    Select the region closest to your application servers to minimise latency. Available regions are listed in the dropdown alongside their approximate ping from common locations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Deploy">
    Click **Deploy**. VenixCloud provisions your database instance within seconds. Once the status indicator turns green, your database is ready to accept connections.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Connection Strings

After deployment, your connection string is available on the service's **Overview** tab. Copy the string that matches your engine and paste it directly into your application's configuration.

<CodeGroup>
```text MySQL
mysql://user:password@host:3306/dbname
```

```text PostgreSQL
postgresql://user:password@host:5432/dbname
```

```text MongoDB
mongodb://user:password@host:27017/dbname
```

```text Redis
redis://:password@host:6379
```
</CodeGroup>

Replace `user`, `password`, `host`, and `dbname` with the values shown on your service's Overview tab. VenixCloud also provides these as individual environment variables (`DB_HOST`, `DB_PORT`, `DB_USER`, `DB_PASS`, `DB_NAME`) if you prefer to construct the string yourself.

## Backups

VenixCloud automatically creates a full backup of your database every day at 02:00 UTC. Backups are stored in an isolated, geographically redundant object store — separate from your primary instance — so a regional outage never affects your recovery options.

Backup retention depends on your plan:

| Plan       | Retention Period |
|------------|-----------------|
| Starter    | 3 days          |
| Pro        | 14 days         |
| Business   | 30 days         |
| Enterprise | 90 days         |

To restore a backup, navigate to your database service, open the **Backups** tab, and click **Restore** next to the snapshot you need.

## Scaling

To upgrade your database's CPU or RAM, go to your service's **Settings** tab and click **Change Plan**. VenixCloud performs the resize with a brief rolling restart — typically under 30 seconds — so downtime is minimal. Disk storage can be increased independently from the **Storage** section of the same settings page; storage upgrades are applied live without any restart.

<Note>
  Each plan enforces a maximum number of simultaneous connections to your database. If your application opens many connections concurrently, consider using a connection pooler such as PgBouncer (PostgreSQL) or ProxySQL (MySQL), or upgrade to a higher plan for a larger connection limit.
</Note>
