The VenixCloud dashboard is your central control plane for everything you host — databases, bots, websites, and custom applications. From a single interface you can spin up new services, monitor running workloads, inspect logs, manage environment variables, and control deployments without ever leaving your browser.

## Dashboard Overview

When you log in, the dashboard surfaces four main areas that give you an at-a-glance picture of your infrastructure.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Services List" icon="server">
    A live inventory of every service in your account, showing its name, type, current status, and region.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Recent Deployments" icon="rocket">
    A feed of the latest deployment events across all services, including timestamps, triggering users, and pass/fail status.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Usage Stats" icon="chart-bar">
    Aggregate CPU, memory, and bandwidth consumption rolled up across your plan, updated every 60 seconds.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Quick Actions" icon="bolt">
    One-click controls to start, stop, restart, or redeploy any service without navigating into its detail view.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Services List

The Services list is the primary way to find and act on any resource in your account.

- **View all services** — all services are displayed in a table sorted by last-updated time by default.
- **Filter by type** — use the **Type** dropdown to show only databases, bots, websites, or custom applications.
- **Filter by status** — the **Status** filter lets you narrow the list to Running, Stopped, or Failed services instantly.
- **Search** — type any part of a service name or ID into the search bar to jump directly to a match. The search is case-insensitive and updates results as you type.

## Service Detail View

Click any service name in the list to open its detail view. This page brings together everything you need to understand and operate that service.

| Section | What you'll find |
|---|---|
| **Status** | Current state (Running, Stopped, Failed), uptime percentage, and last deployment timestamp. |
| **Metrics** | Live CPU, RAM, and network graphs updated every 30 seconds. |
| **Logs** | A streaming log tail showing stdout, stderr, and system events. |
| **Environment** | All configured environment variables (values masked by default). |
| **Settings** | Service name, region, scaling options, and deletion controls. |

## Quick Actions

You can manage the lifecycle of any service without opening its detail view. Each row in the Services list exposes an actions menu (the **⋮** icon on the right).

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the actions menu">
    Hover over a service row and click the **⋮** icon on the far right to reveal the actions menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose an action">
    Select one of the available operations:
    - **Start** — boots a stopped service and begins routing traffic.
    - **Stop** — gracefully shuts down the service, preserving its configuration.
    - **Restart** — stops and immediately starts the service; useful for applying config changes.
    - **Redeploy** — triggers a fresh build and deployment using the latest image or commit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Destructive actions (Stop, Restart) display a confirmation prompt. Click **Confirm** to proceed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Settings

Account-wide settings are accessible from the **Settings** link in the left sidebar.

- **Profile** — update your display name and email address.
- **API Keys** — generate, rotate, or revoke API keys used to authenticate programmatic requests.
- **Plan Management** — view your current plan limits, upgrade to a higher tier, or review billing history.
- **Team** — invite collaborators and assign roles (Owner, Editor, Viewer) on paid plans.

<Tip>
  For bulk operations — such as redeploying dozens of services at once or scripting environment changes — use the [VenixCloud REST API](https://api.venixcloud.com/docs) instead of the dashboard. API keys are available under **Settings → API Keys**.
</Tip>
