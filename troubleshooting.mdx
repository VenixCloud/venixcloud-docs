When something goes wrong on VenixCloud, the fastest path to a fix is checking your service logs first. Most issues leave a clear error message in the build or runtime output. Use the sections below to identify your problem and follow the recommended steps to resolve it — and if you're still stuck, reach out to the support team at the bottom of this page.

## Deployments

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="My deployment is stuck on 'Building'">
    A build that never completes usually points to one of the following:

    - **Check your build logs** — open the [Logs tab](/management/logs-monitoring) on your service dashboard and look for the last line output before it stalled.
    - **Verify your build command** — confirm the command in your service settings (e.g. `npm run build`) is correct and actually exits when finished.
    - **Check your dependencies** — make sure all packages listed in your `package.json`, `requirements.txt`, or equivalent file are available and correctly versioned.

    If the build has been running for more than 15 minutes with no output, cancel it and redeploy. If the issue persists, contact support with your deployment ID.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Deployment failed with exit code 1">
    Exit code 1 means your application process terminated with an error. Here's how to diagnose it:

    - **Read stdout/stderr** — open the [Logs tab](/management/logs-monitoring) and scroll to the bottom of the failed deployment to find the actual error message.
    - **Check for missing environment variables** — a common cause is a required env var (like `DATABASE_URL` or `SECRET_KEY`) not being set. Go to [Settings → Environment](/management/environment-variables) and verify all required variables are present.
    - **Verify your start command** — make sure the command in your service settings points to the correct entry file (e.g. `node src/index.js` or `python main.py`).
    - **Check the port** — if your app is a web service, ensure it binds to the `PORT` environment variable provided by VenixCloud.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My app deployed but returns 502 Bad Gateway">
    A 502 error means VenixCloud's routing layer reached your container but your application wasn't responding. This typically happens because:

    - Your app crashed immediately after startup — check the [Runtime Logs](/management/logs-monitoring) for a stack trace or panic message.
    - Your app is listening on the wrong port — VenixCloud injects a `PORT` environment variable. Make sure your server binds to `process.env.PORT` (Node.js) or `int(os.environ.get("PORT"))` (Python) rather than a hard-coded value like `3000` or `8080`.
    - Your app takes too long to start — if startup exceeds the health-check timeout, VenixCloud marks the service as unhealthy. Optimize your initialization code or increase the startup grace period in service settings.

    <Tip>
      Add a lightweight health-check endpoint (e.g. `GET /health` returning `200 OK`) so VenixCloud can verify your app is ready before routing traffic to it.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Databases

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Cannot connect to my database">
    If your application can't reach its database, work through these checks in order:

    1. **Verify your connection string** — open [Settings → Environment](/management/environment-variables) and confirm the `DATABASE_URL` (or equivalent) is copied exactly from the [database service's](/services/databases) **Connection** tab. Watch for typos or missing special characters.
    2. **Check the database service status** — go to your database service in the dashboard and confirm it shows a **Running** status. If it shows **Stopped** or **Error**, restart it and review its logs.
    3. **Review access settings** — if you're connecting from an external client (e.g. a local tool like DBeaver), make sure the database's firewall rules allow your IP address. Internal service-to-service connections within VenixCloud don't require firewall changes.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Too many connections error">
    Most database engines enforce a maximum number of simultaneous connections. If you're hitting this limit:

    - **Upgrade your plan** — higher-tier plans on VenixCloud allow more concurrent connections. Go to [Settings → Plan](/plans-billing) to compare options.
    - **Use a connection pooler** — tools like [PgBouncer](https://www.pgbouncer.org/) (PostgreSQL) or [ProxySQL](https://proxysql.com/) (MySQL) sit between your app and the database and multiplex many application connections over a smaller number of real database connections.
    - **Audit your application code** — make sure your app releases connections back to the pool after each request and doesn't leak open connections on errors.

    <Note>
      If you're using an ORM like Prisma or SQLAlchemy, consult its documentation on configuring the connection pool size to avoid exhausting available connections.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Bots

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="My bot goes offline randomly">
    Intermittent disconnects are almost always caused by unhandled exceptions crashing the bot process. VenixCloud automatically restarts crashed services, which is why the bot comes back — but you should fix the root cause:

    - **Read the crash logs** — open the [Logs tab](/management/logs-monitoring) and look for the stack trace that appeared just before the restart. The error message will tell you exactly what failed.
    - **Add global error handling** — wrap your bot's event handlers in try/catch blocks (Node.js) or use a top-level `except` handler (Python) so a single bad event doesn't bring down the whole process.
    - **Check for memory leaks** — if the bot runs fine for hours then crashes, check memory usage in the **Metrics** tab. A steady climb in memory over time indicates a leak.

    For more on keeping your bot online, see the [Bots service guide](/services/bots).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Bot token invalid error">
    This error means the platform (Discord, Telegram, etc.) rejected the token your bot is using to authenticate. To fix it:

    - **Check the `BOT_TOKEN` environment variable** — go to [Settings → Environment](/management/environment-variables) and confirm the variable is set. Make sure there are no leading or trailing spaces — these are invisible but will invalidate the token.
    - **Regenerate the token** — if the token was accidentally shared or committed to a public repository, regenerate it immediately in your bot application's developer portal and update the env var in VenixCloud.
    - **Confirm the correct variable name** — make sure your code reads the token from the same variable name you've defined (e.g. `process.env.BOT_TOKEN` or `os.environ["BOT_TOKEN"]`).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Billing

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="My service was suspended">
    Services are suspended for one of two reasons:

    - **Payment failed** — your card was declined or expired. Go to [Settings → Billing](/plans-billing) to update your payment method and settle any outstanding balance. Your service will be reactivated automatically once the payment is processed.
    - **Plan limits exceeded** — your service consumed more resources (RAM, CPU, bandwidth) than your current plan allows. Go to [Settings → Plan](/plans-billing) to upgrade to a tier that covers your usage.

    <Note>
      VenixCloud sends warning emails before suspending a service. Check your inbox (including spam) for prior notifications.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I was charged twice">
    Duplicate charges are rare but can occasionally occur due to payment processor retries. To resolve it:

    1. Go to [Settings → Billing → Invoices](/plans-billing) and locate both charge entries.
    2. Note the **Invoice IDs** for both transactions.
    3. Contact the support team (see below) with both invoice IDs and the charge amounts.

    The team will investigate and issue a refund for the duplicate charge within 3–5 business days.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Contact Support

If none of the steps above resolved your issue, the VenixCloud support team is ready to help. Include your **service name**, **deployment ID** (visible in the URL of your service dashboard), and any relevant **log excerpts** to get a faster resolution.
