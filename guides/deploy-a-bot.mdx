This guide walks you through deploying a Discord bot on VenixCloud from scratch. Whether your bot is written in Node.js or Python, you'll have it running 24/7 with automatic restarts and live log monitoring in just a few minutes.

## Prerequisites

- A Discord bot token from the [Discord Developer Portal](https://discord.com/developers/applications)
- A Node.js or Python bot project ready to deploy
- A VenixCloud account — [sign up here](https://venixcloud.app)

## Deploy Your Bot

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare your bot">
    Make sure your bot's entry point is a single file — `index.js` for Node.js or `main.py` for Python. Your bot must read its token from an environment variable rather than hardcoding it in source code.

    <CodeGroup>
      ```js Node.js
      const token = process.env.BOT_TOKEN;
      client.login(token);
      ```

      ```python Python
      import os
      bot.run(os.environ['BOT_TOKEN'])
      ```
    </CodeGroup>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create the service">
    In your VenixCloud Dashboard, click **New Service** and select **Bot** as the service type. Choose either **Node.js** or **Python** as your runtime depending on your project.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload or connect your repo">
    Connect your **GitHub repository** for automatic deployments on every push, or upload your project as a **ZIP file** if you prefer a manual workflow. VenixCloud will detect your project structure automatically.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add environment variables">
    Navigate to the **Environment** tab of your service and add the following variable:

    | Key | Value |
    |-----|-------|
    | `BOT_TOKEN` | Your Discord bot token |

    Your bot token is now securely injected at runtime and never stored in your code.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the start command">
    Under **Settings**, set the start command that VenixCloud uses to launch your bot:

    - **Node.js**: `node index.js`
    - **Python**: `python main.py`

    Adjust the filename if your entry point has a different name.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Deploy">
    Click the **Deploy** button. VenixCloud will install your dependencies, build your project if needed, and start your bot. Wait for the service status indicator to turn green and display **Running**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify your bot is online">
    Open the **Logs** tab on your service to view real-time output. You should see your bot's ready message — for example, `Logged in as MyBot#1234`. Check your Discord server to confirm the bot is online and responding to commands.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If your bot connects to a database or uses external APIs, add those credentials as additional environment variables on the same **Environment** tab — for example, `DATABASE_URL` or `API_KEY`. Keep all secrets out of your source code.
</Tip>

<Note>
  VenixCloud automatically restarts your bot if it crashes unexpectedly. You can configure the restart policy and view crash history in the **Settings** tab of your service.
</Note>
